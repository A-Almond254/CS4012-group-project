Japanese Cuisines

Home Travel Plans Tourist Attractions Japanese Cuisines

Japan offers an incredible culinary adventure for tourists, with dishes that span from comforting street snacks to refined gourmet experiences. Travelers can enjoy authentic sushi and sashimi prepared with pristine seasonal fish, steamy bowls of ramen in countless styles, and flavorful yakitori skewers grilled over charcoal. Regional specialties like Osaka’s savory okonomiyaki and Hiroshima’s unique layered version add variety, while Kyoto’s traditional kaiseki dining introduces delicate, beautifully presented courses rooted in centuries-old culinary tradition. Whether sampling tempura, slurping udon or soba noodles, or treating themselves to sweet mochi and matcha desserts, visitors will find that every meal in Japan is a celebration of craftsmanship, freshness, and flavor.


A quick list on the many different culinary choices you have in Japan

Casual / Street Styled Dishes

  • Takoyaki: crisp dough balls filled with octopus, topped with sauce, mayo, and bonito flakes.
  • Okonomiyaki: a savory “Japanese pancake,” with toppings mixed in; especially famous in Osaka and Hiroshima.
  • Taiyaki: a fish-shaped pastry usually filled with red bean, custard, or chocolate.
  • Onigiri: rice balls wrapped in seaweed with fillings like tuna mayo, pickled plum, or grilled salmon.

Traditional Dishes

  • Kaiseki: Japanese haute cuisine: multiple small, carefully balanced seasonal dishes.
  • Unagi (grilled eel): rich, smoky, and slightly sweet, served over rice.
  • Soba: buckwheat noodles served hot or chilled, subtle and nutty in flavor.
  • Shabu-shabu: thinly sliced meat and vegetables swirled through a hot pot before dipping in sauces.

Unique Dishes

  • Natto: fermented soybeans with a sticky texture and strong flavor, beloved by many locals.
  • Fugu (pufferfish): prepared by licensed chefs; prized for its delicate taste.
  • Uni (sea urchin); creamy and briny, adored in sushi culture.
  • Basashi (horse sashimi) — thin-sliced raw horse meat, particularly associated with Kumamoto.
